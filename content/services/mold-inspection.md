## Environmental Consultant — Why It Matters

Finding mold is not the skill — it's the starting point. As an IAC2 certified environmental consultant, our job is to understand why the mold is there. What is the moisture source? How long has it been active? How far has it spread from the visible area? Is the HVAC system distributing spores to other rooms? These questions determine whether a mold problem is straightforward to resolve or something more involved — and without answering them, remediation addresses the surface while leaving the cause, which means the mold returns.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between a mold inspection and mold testing?

A mold inspection is the physical and visual assessment of the property — using moisture meters, thermal imaging, and professional observation to evaluate building conditions and identify mold growth or moisture sources. Mold testing adds laboratory analysis of air or surface samples to quantify and identify mold species. Inspection is always the first step; testing is added when the inspection raises questions that visual evidence alone can't answer.

### How long does a mold inspection take?

Most single-family homes require 2–3 hours for a thorough inspection. Larger homes, properties with accessible attics and crawlspaces, or homes with multiple areas of concern take longer. We don't reduce inspection time to fit a schedule — thoroughness is the core of what we're delivering.

If remediation is needed, we provide clear documentation and can refer qualified professionals.

### Can mold make you sick?

Exposure to elevated mold concentrations can cause respiratory irritation, allergic reactions, sinus symptoms, headaches, and fatigue in sensitive individuals. Certain mold species produce mycotoxins that have more serious health implications. We provide the environmental data that physicians and patients need to make informed decisions — we don't diagnose illness.
