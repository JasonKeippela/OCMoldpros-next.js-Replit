## Physical and Visual Inspection

A physical and visual inspection is exactly what it sounds like: we inspect the home like a home inspector should, but with a special focus on moisture, building leaks, and mold risk.

We look at things like:
(THIS MUST BE A JSX BULLET LIST)
- Bathrooms: shower corners, grout lines, caulk breaks, fans, under-sink plumbing
- Kitchen: sink base, dishwasher area, fridge water line area, staining or swelling
- Bedrooms: closets on exterior walls, window condensation patterns, musty zones
- Windows/sliders: staining, soft drywall edges, failed seals, drafts that carry moisture
- Attic (if accessible): roof penetrations, bath vent terminations, staining, insulation gaps
- Crawlspace (if accessible): damp soil, standing water signs, basic venting and vapor barrier issues
- HVAC basics: visible moisture around registers, drain line issues, obvious airflow problems
- Exterior: gutters, downspouts, grading, cracks or gaps that let water in

We also talk through your history: past leaks, remodeling, odors, and any “this only happens in winter” patterns.

---

## What we find most often in San Clemente homes

Coastal air is real. Marine layer is real. And older homes near the coast can have small water entry points that slowly create big issues.

Common patterns we see:
(THIS MUST BE A JSX BULLET LIST)
- Bathroom fans that don’t move enough air
- Showers with old caulk and tiny gaps that let water behind tile
- Window condensation that keeps feeding mold in corners
- Closet odors on exterior walls with low airflow
- Gutters overflowing in winter rains and wetting the same wall over and over
- “Repaired” leaks where the source got fixed but the material stayed wet

## Do you need sampling after a physical and visual inspection?

Sometimes yes, sometimes no.

If we see strong indicators (staining, moisture patterns, musty odor zones), mold sampling can help confirm what’s in the air or on a surface. If everything looks normal, sometimes the best next step is prevention work and moisture control.

We’ll tell you straight what makes sense.
