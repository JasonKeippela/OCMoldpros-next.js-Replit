## HVAC System Inspection

We’re not doing a full mechanical tear-down like an HVAC contractor, but we do a solid inspection focused on moisture, visible condition, and red flags that affect air quality.

During an HVAC system inspection, we typically look at:
(THIS MUST BE A JSX BULLET LIST)
- Air handler / furnace cabinet condition (visible dust, staining, rust, microbial-looking growth)
- Evaporator coil area (signs of buildup, wetness, access panel condition)
- Condensate drain line routing and basic function (clogs, improper slope, overflow risk)
- Drain pan condition (standing water, corrosion, slime buildup)
- Filter size and fit (gaps that bypass the filter, wrong MERV use if obvious)
- Supply and return vents (visible dust patterns, moisture staining near registers)
- Basic airflow signs (weak returns, blocked registers, obvious imbalance)
- Attic ducting (if accessible): disconnected ducts, crushed flex, sweating duct sections
- Thermostat operation basics (if the template already includes this, keep it consistent)

If we see strong moisture issues, we’ll tell you what needs HVAC contractor attention vs what’s more of a home moisture problem.

## Why HVAC matters so much for mold risk

Mold needs moisture. HVAC systems create moisture by nature (condensation). If the drain line clogs or the pan holds water, you can get microbial growth fast.

Common HVAC-related problems we see:
(THIS MUST BE A JSX BULLET LIST)
- Clogged condensate drains causing overflow
- Wet or dirty coils that stay damp too long
- Drain pans holding water
- Ducts sweating in attics or crawlspaces
- Filters that don’t fit right (air bypass)
- Returns pulling dusty or damp air from bad locations
